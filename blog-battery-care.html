How to Take Care of Your E-Scooter Battery and
Make It Last Longer

Your e-scooter's battery is its most important — and most expensive — component. A replacement can cost ₹8,000–₹20,000 depending on the model. With the right habits, a well-maintained battery can last 3–5 years instead of just 1–2. Here are 6 tips our service team at Expreso recommends to every customer.

📊
Tip 1
Never Let the Battery Drop Below 20%

Consistently draining your battery to 0% causes irreversible damage to the battery cells — this is called "deep discharge". Try to plug in for charging when the indicator shows around 20–25%. Modern e-scooters often have a low-battery warning light — don't ignore it.

🔌
Tip 2
Don't Overcharge — Remove at 100%

Leaving your scooter plugged in all night after it reaches 100% puts unnecessary stress on the battery cells. Once charging is complete, unplug it. If you charge overnight, try to estimate the time needed and set an alarm if possible. Avoid "top-up charging" repeatedly — charge in one full cycle.

🌡️
Tip 3
Charge in a Cool, Shaded Place

Heat is a battery's worst enemy. Never charge your e-scooter in direct sunlight or in a closed room that gets very hot. Ideal charging temperature is 15–30°C. In West Bengal summers, charge early in the morning or in the evening when temperatures are lower. Overheating during charging can permanently reduce battery capacity.

🔋
Tip 4
Always Use the Original Charger from Expreso

Third-party chargers may be cheaper, but they often deliver incorrect voltage or current — causing battery cells to degrade faster or even fail. Always use the original charger that came with your vehicle, or purchase a genuine replacement from Expreso. Our spare parts counter in Uluberia stocks all original chargers.

🔧
Tip 5
Get a Full Service Every 3 Months at Expreso

Our service team performs a full battery health check during every scheduled service — checking cell voltage, connections, temperature sensors, and charge cycle count. Early detection of a degrading cell can save you thousands in replacement costs. Book your service on WhatsApp — we'll come to you or you can visit our Uluberia showroom.

📦
Tip 6
Store at 50% Charge if Not Using for 2+ Weeks

If your scooter will be unused for an extended period (travel, rainy season, etc.), don't leave the battery fully charged or fully empty. Store it at approximately 50% charge in a cool, dry place. Check on it once a month and top up to 50% if needed. This significantly extends long-term battery health.

⚠️ Warning: If you notice your battery heating up unusually, smelling strange, or showing swelling on the casing — stop using the vehicle immediately and contact Expreso on +91 91639 68140. Never ignore battery warning signs.

The Bottom Line

A well-maintained e-scooter battery can easily last 3–5 years with 80%+ of its original capacity. These habits cost nothing extra — just awareness and a little discipline. Follow these 6 tips and you'll avoid expensive battery replacements and keep your scooter performing at its best.

Top 5 Benefits of E-Rickshaw for Indian Roads

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ Expreso Team 👁 1,247 views
🛺

Electric rickshaws — commonly called e-rickshaws or e-totos — have quietly revolutionised last-mile transportation across India, especially in West Bengal. With over 1.5 million e-rickshaws now operating across the country, they're no longer just an alternative — they're the smart choice for drivers and passengers alike.

At Expreso, we've helped over 500 drivers make the switch to electric. Here are the top 5 benefits we see every day:

1. Zero Fuel Cost = Maximum Daily Earnings 💰

The biggest advantage of an e-rickshaw is simple: you pay nothing for fuel. A full charge costs approximately ₹15–20 of electricity for 80–110 km of range. Compare that to a CNG or petrol auto which burns ₹300–400 per day in fuel.

Our drivers report net daily savings of ₹280–380 compared to petrol vehicles — that's over ₹8,000–11,000 per month that goes directly into their pocket.

💡 Real Customer Example
Ratan Das from Uluberia purchased a Saarthi Plus in January 2025. He earns ₹1,200/day on average and his electricity cost is under ₹20/day. After EMI of ₹3,400/month, he nets over ₹25,000 per month — double what he earned with his old petrol auto.

2. Lower Maintenance, Fewer Breakdowns 🔧

E-rickshaws have far fewer moving parts than petrol or CNG vehicles. There's no engine oil, no gearbox, no complex combustion system. The main components are:

  • BLDC Hub Motor (virtually zero wear)
  • Lithium-Ion or Lead-Acid Battery
  • Controller (ECU)
  • Basic brake and suspension

Most of our customers service their vehicles just once every 3–4 months, costing ₹500–800 per visit. Petrol autos require weekly maintenance averaging ₹1,500–2,000 per month.

3. Government Support and Subsidies 🏛️

The Indian government actively promotes EV adoption through:

  • FAME II Scheme: Direct subsidy on purchase of e-rickshaws
  • West Bengal EV Policy: Road tax exemption and registration fee waiver
  • Bank Priority Lending: Easier loan access for EV buyers under RBI guidelines
  • Green Number Plates: Preferred access lanes in some cities

4. Better Passenger Experience = More Repeat Customers 😊

E-rickshaws are quieter, smoother, and cleaner than petrol options. Passengers prefer them because there's no exhaust smell, less vibration, and a more comfortable ride. This leads to better tips, more repeat customers, and higher daily trip counts.

"My passengers actively look for my e-rickshaw because it's so much more comfortable. I make more per day and they're happier. It's a win-win." — Monika Ghosh, Expreso Customer, Moubesia

5. Eco-Friendly = Better India 🌱

Each e-rickshaw replaces approximately 2.5 tonnes of CO₂ emissions per year compared to a petrol auto. With lakhs of e-rickshaws on Indian roads, the collective environmental impact is enormous. You're not just running a business — you're building a cleaner future for your children.

West Bengal's urban areas have seen measurable improvements in air quality in areas where e-rickshaw adoption is high, particularly in Howrah and Kolkata.
